Anime Creators

Eiichiro Oda

Eiichiro Oda was born on January 1, 1975, in Kumamoto, Japan. He said that at the age of four he resolved to become a manga artist in order to avoid having to get a "real job". His biggest influence is Akira Toriyama and his series Dragon Ball.

Mashashi Kishimoto

a Japanese manga artist. His manga series, Naruto, which was in serialization from 1999 to 2014, has sold over 250 million copies worldwide in 46 countries as of May 2019.

Ken Sugimori

a Japanese video game designer, illustrator, manga artist, and director. He is best known as the primary character designer and art director for the Pokémon franchise. Sugimori is also credited with the art direction for other titles, including Pulseman.

Akira Toriyama

a Japanese manga writer, manga artist, and character designer for video games. He has been a working artist since 1978. In manga, he is better known for creating the science fiction comedy series "Dr. Slump" (1980-1984) and the martial-arts-themed series "Dragon Ball" (1984-1995).

Hajime Isayama

Hajime Isayama is a Japanese manga artist. His first series, Attack on Titan, became one of the best-selling manga series of all time with 110 million copies in circulation as of September 2022.

Hideaki Anno

Hideaki Anno is a Japanese animator, filmmaker and actor. He is best known for creating the anime series Neon Genesis Evangelion.

Tite Kubo

Noriaki Kubo, known professionally as Tite Kubo, is a Japanese manga artist and character designer. He is best known for his manga series Bleach, which had over 130 million copies in circulation as of 2022.

Yusuke Murata

A Japanese manga artist and animator, best known for illustrating One's One-Punch Man, serialized in the Weekly Young Jump online version. Murata's other major work is his illustration of the American football manga Eyeshield 21, in collaboration with writer Riichiro Inagaki.

Hiromu Arakawa

Hiromu Arakawa is a Japanese manga artist. She is best known for the manga series Fullmetal Alchemist, which became a hit both domestically and internationally, and was adapted into two anime television series. She is also known for Silver Spoon and the manga adaptation of The Heroic Legend of Arslan novels.

A style of animation originating in Japan that is characterized by stark colorful graphics depicting vibrant characters in action-filled plots often with fantastic or futuristic themes.

The main reason why anime became so popular is because it is versatile. It can be used to teach different subjects and can be used to tell different stories. People started watching anime because of their love for the art form and this popularity has continued to grow over the years.

Anime is hand-drawn and computer-generated animation originating from Japan. Outside of Japan and in English, anime refers specifically to animation produced in Japan. However, in Japan and in Japanese, anime describes all animated works, regardless of style or origin.
